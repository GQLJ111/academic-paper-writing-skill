# Target Journal Fit

Use this reference when adapting a manuscript to a journal family or deciding what writing style the paper should follow. Do not invent target-journal requirements; use this as a heuristic unless the journal guide is provided.

## 1. Fit Questions

Before revising for journal fit, identify:

```text
Target journal or journal family:
Main contribution type:
Evidence type:
Expected reader:
Required level of methodological rigor:
Expected article length:
What the journal likely values:
What the journal likely rejects:
```

## 2. Transport / Automation Journal Families

### Transportation Research Part C

Likely values:

- Intelligent transportation systems, automation, connected vehicles, emerging mobility.
- Strong mechanism, modeling, simulation, control, data, or system-level insight.
- Clear relation between behavior assumptions and traffic/system outcomes.

Writing emphasis:

- Make the transport/automation mechanism explicit.
- Explain why findings differ from prior AV/CAV literature.
- Keep scenarios and behavioral assumptions transparent.

Avoid:

- Treating SUMO parameter scenarios as real-world predictions.
- Reporting many simulations without mechanism.

### Transportation Research Part B

Likely values:

- Theory, methodology, modeling rigor, analytical contribution.
- Strong problem formulation, equations/notation when needed, assumptions, validation, and generalizable insight.

Writing emphasis:

- Clarify model structure, assumptions, and why the method advances knowledge.
- Define key equations, symbols, units, objective functions, or constraints when they carry the method contribution.
- Use experiments to validate a method or theory, not just to report cases.

Avoid:

- A purely descriptive case study with weak methodological novelty.

### IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems / IEEE ITS Journals

Likely values:

- Algorithms, systems, control, sensing, communication, optimization, implementation.
- Engineering validation, robustness, comparative baselines.

Writing emphasis:

- Present method/system architecture clearly.
- Include baselines, ablation, sensitivity, and robustness.
- Make technical contribution explicit.

Avoid:

- Long narrative literature review without technical payoff.

### Accident Analysis & Prevention

Likely values:

- Safety, human factors, crash risk, surrogate safety measures, behavioral evidence.
- Careful experimental design and cautious interpretation.

Writing emphasis:

- Define safety metrics precisely.
- Avoid equating traffic efficiency with safety.
- Discuss behavioral and risk mechanisms.

Avoid:

- Strong safety claims from limited proxy metrics.

### Transportation Research Record / Transportation Letters

Likely values:

- Applied transportation evidence, practical modeling, case studies, policy/operations insight.

Writing emphasis:

- Clear problem, reproducible method, practical interpretation.
- Less theoretical ambition is acceptable if evidence is useful and scoped.

Avoid:

- Overstated novelty or overly broad generalization.

### Sustainability / Applied Sciences / Broad-Scope Journals

Likely values:

- Complete structure, interdisciplinary framing, application context, accessible methods.

Writing emphasis:

- Clear motivation, full method, readable results, practical implications.

Avoid:

- Overly long generic background.
- Weak link between results and sustainability/application claims.

## 3. Contribution-Type Fit

| Contribution type | Best-fit emphasis |
|---|---|
| New mechanism insight | TRC-style mechanism and scenario comparison |
| New traffic model/control method | TRB or IEEE-style formulation and validation |
| Empirical AV/HDV behavior evidence | TRC, AAP, or data-focused transport journal |
| Safety-focused mixed traffic analysis | AAP or safety-oriented transport journal |
| Applied SUMO scenario evaluation | TRR, Transportation Letters, broad applied journal |
| Full thesis chapter | Thesis structure first; later compress to journal form |

## 4. Adaptation Rules

- For mechanism journals, reduce generic background and strengthen causal/mechanism logic.
- For methods journals, move problem formulation and method novelty earlier.
- For applied journals, make scenario design, reproducibility, and practical implications clearer.
- For safety journals, narrow efficiency claims and expand safety metric validity.
- For broad journals, keep structure accessible but remove filler.
